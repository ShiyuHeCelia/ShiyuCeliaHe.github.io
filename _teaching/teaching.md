## Teaching profile

My teaching focuses on **research methods, experimental design, and quantitative skills** in psychology and linguistics, with particular expertise in **eye-tracking methodologies** and reproducible data analysis.

## Teaching Technician  
**University of Bristol**  
*Aug 2024 – Present*

I provide teaching and research support across undergraduate and postgraduate programmes in the School of Psychological Science, with a strong emphasis on **methods training and experimental practice**.

### Research methods & experimental design
- Teaching **experimental design in psychology**, including within- and between-subjects designs, counterbalancing, and randomisation
- Hands-on training in experiment-building platforms:
  - **Gorilla**
  - **Qualtrics**
  - **Experiment Builder** (eye-tracking)
- Supporting eye-tracking studies using **EyeLink** systems, including task setup, calibration, and data quality control

### Quantitative & statistical training
- Teaching and supporting data analysis in:
  - **R** (tidyverse, mixed-effects models, reproducible workflows)
  - **SPSS**
  - **G*Power** (power analysis and study planning)
- Advising students on statistical decision-making and transparent reporting

### Research governance & good practice
- Training students and staff in:
  - **Research risk assessments**
  - **Health and safety procedures**
- Ethical and practical considerations in experimental research
- Supporting the development of safe, compliant, and well-documented research projects
